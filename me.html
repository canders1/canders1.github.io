About

I am currently a PhD candidate in Linguistics at UMass Amherst.

I graduated from Swarthmore College in 2014 with a major in Linguistics and a minor in Computer Science.

I study perspective in language: the ways in which people's use of language depends on their point-of-view. This includes context-sensitivity in expressions that describe space and time as well as the ways in which people express their beliefs and opinions.

My work focuses on using experimental and computational methods to understand how people express and track perspective across discourses and conversations. I use Bayesian inference techniques to model reasoning about perspective in conversation, and construct production and comprehension experiments to gather empirical evidence.

I am also affiliated with the Speech and Language group at BBN Technologies, where I've worked on several topics in automatic speech recognition.
